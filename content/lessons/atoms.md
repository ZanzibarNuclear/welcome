# Atomic Structure Basics

Welcome to your first lesson on atomic structure! By the end of this lesson, you'll understand what atoms are made of and how they're organized.

## What is an Atom?

An atom is the smallest unit of matter that retains the properties of an element. Every element on the periodic table is defined by its atomic structure.

## The Three Main Parts

### 1. Protons

- Located in the nucleus (center)
- Positively charged (+1)
- Define the element (atomic number)
- Mass: approximately 1 atomic mass unit (amu)

### 2. Neutrons

- Also in the nucleus
- No electric charge (neutral)
- Add mass but don't change the element
- Mass: approximately 1 amu

### 3. Electrons

- Orbit the nucleus in shells/orbitals
- Negatively charged (-1)
- Determine chemical behavior
- Mass: about 1/2000th of a proton (negligible)

## Atomic Number and Mass

- **Atomic Number** = number of protons (defines the element)
- **Mass Number** = protons + neutrons
- **Charge** = protons - electrons (neutral atom has equal numbers)

## Example: Carbon

Let's look at a carbon atom (C):

- Atomic number: 6 (6 protons)
- Mass number: 12 (6 protons + 6 neutrons)
- Electrons: 6 (in a neutral atom)

## Key Takeaways

1. Atoms are made of protons, neutrons, and electrons
2. Protons and neutrons form the dense nucleus
3. Electrons orbit in shells around the nucleus
4. The number of protons defines what element it is
5. Atoms are mostly empty space!
