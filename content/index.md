# Everything is Atoms

The entire universe is made of atoms. You might say atoms are the building blocks of stuff.

## The Building Blocks of Matter

Are atoms the smallest bits of stuff? No. Atoms are built from nuclei: neutrons, protons, and electrons. Nuclei, in turn, are built from so-called "particles."

- Quarks
- Leptons
- Bosons

Particles may be made of even smaller bits, but we haven't seen them yet.

### Observable Elements

On the other hand, atoms are the smallest bits of stuff that you can recognize from our daily experience:

- **Hydrogen** burns
- **Helium** floats balloons and makes your voice sound squeaky
- **Lithium** explodes in water

We could go all the way through the periodic table and describe each element—how they look, smell, and behave.

## Molecules: A Different Kind of Building Block

When you combine atoms into molecules, you get different stuff with different properties. A molecule of water is composed of three atoms: two hydrogens and an oxygen. So a molecule is another "smallest bit of stuff."

Can you pour yourself a glass of free-floating quarks? Nope. How about a nice lepton biscuit? Not happening.
