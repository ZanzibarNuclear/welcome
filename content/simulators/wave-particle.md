# Wave-Particle Simulator

Experience the famous double-slit experiment that demonstrates wave-particle duality—one of the most mind-bending phenomena in quantum mechanics.

## The Double-Slit Experiment

Fire particles (like electrons or photons) at a barrier with two slits. When you're not observing which slit they go through, they create an interference pattern like waves. When you observe them, they behave like particles.

## What You'll Observe

### Wave Behavior
- Interference patterns
- Multiple paths simultaneously
- Probability waves

### Particle Behavior
- Discrete impacts
- Single paths
- The "observer effect"

## Controls

- **Particle Type**: Choose electrons, photons, or other particles
- **Observation**: Toggle observation on/off to see how it affects the pattern
- **Firing Rate**: Control how many particles are sent
- **Reset**: Clear the screen and start over

## Questions to Explore

1. What pattern appears when you don't observe the particles?
2. What happens when you turn observation on?
3. Can particles really be in two places at once?
4. What does this mean for the nature of reality?

## The Quantum Mystery

This experiment shows that observation itself affects reality at the quantum scale. It's not that we're disturbing the particles—the act of measurement fundamentally changes how they behave.
