## What this viewer shows

This simulator renders a **2D slice (z = 0)** of selected hydrogenic orbitals (s, p, and d examples). Colors represent either probability density only, or phase sign + density.

## Why this is useful

- See **lobes** and **nodes** directly
- Compare different orbital families quickly
- Build intuition before moving to full 3D or many-electron models

## Modeling scope

The equations are hydrogenic forms in atomic units, suitable for teaching intuition. This is not a full many-electron Hartree-Fock or DFT solver.
