# Pressurized Water Reactor

A pressurized water reactor, or PWR, keeps water under high pressure so it can carry heat from the reactor core without boiling. That hot primary water passes through a steam generator, where it heats a separate water loop into steam for a turbine.

## Why It Matters

PWRs are the dominant commercial reactor design worldwide. Their two-loop arrangement helps keep radioactive primary coolant separated from the turbine equipment.
