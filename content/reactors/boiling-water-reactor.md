# Boiling Water Reactor

A boiling water reactor, or BWR, allows water to boil inside the reactor vessel. The steam produced in the core flows directly to the turbine, then condenses and returns to the reactor.

## Why It Matters

BWRs use a simpler steam cycle than pressurized water reactors, but the turbine system must be designed for steam that comes directly from the reactor vessel.
