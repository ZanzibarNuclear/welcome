# RBMK Reactor

RBMK stands for _Reaktor Bolshoy Moshchnosti Kanalnyy_, often translated as "high-power channel-type reactor." It is a Soviet-designed reactor that uses graphite as the moderator and light water as the coolant.

## Why It Matters

The RBMK is historically important because of the 1986 Chernobyl accident, which involved RBMK Unit 4. The design also illustrates how reactor physics, control systems, operating procedures, and safety culture all interact.

## How It Differs

Unlike many light-water power reactors, an RBMK uses pressure tubes instead of one large pressure vessel. Fuel channels run through a large graphite moderator block, and water flows through those channels to remove heat.
