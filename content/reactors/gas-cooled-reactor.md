# Gas-Cooled Reactor

A gas-cooled reactor uses a gas coolant to move heat from the reactor core. Historical designs often used carbon dioxide and graphite moderation, while advanced high-temperature concepts often use helium and specialized fuels.

## Why It Matters

Gas-cooled reactors can operate at high temperatures, which makes them useful for efficient electricity generation and potential industrial heat applications. Some designs also have strong passive safety characteristics because of their fuel form and thermal behavior.
