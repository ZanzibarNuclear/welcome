# The Quantum World: Where Physics Gets Weird

When we zoom into the atomic scale, the rules of classical physics break down. Welcome to the quantum realm, where particles behave in ways that seem impossible.

## Wave-Particle Duality

Light and matter exhibit both wave-like and particle-like properties. An electron isn't just a particle—it's also a wave of probability.

## Superposition

Before measurement, quantum particles exist in all possible states simultaneously. Schrödinger's famous cat thought experiment illustrates this paradox.

## Entanglement

Two particles can become "entangled," instantly affecting each other regardless of distance. Einstein called it "spooky action at a distance."

## Why It Matters

Quantum mechanics isn't just theoretical—it powers:
- Transistors in your computer
- Lasers
- MRI machines
- Emerging quantum computers

The quantum world is strange, but it's the foundation of modern technology.
